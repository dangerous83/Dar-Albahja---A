Part I · Discovery

The brief we were given

الموجز الذي انطلقنا منه

Create a complete visual identity for an authentic Moroccan restaurant — one that feels hand-crafted and soulful, yet disciplined enough to run across signage systems, packaging lines, uniforms and a delivery fleet without losing a single thread of its heritage.

١

Rooted, not costumed أصالة لا تقليد

Real Moroccan craft language — zellige, arabesque, calligraphy — never tourist clichés.

٢

Bilingual by birth ثنائية اللغة

English leads internationally; Arabic is the origin of the name and must live inside the mark itself.

٣

Built to scale قابلة للتوسع

One identity that survives a wax seal, a shopfront, a rollup banner and a motorbike box.

٤

Joy as strategy البهجة استراتيجية

Every touchpoint should feel like being welcomed into a Moroccan home on a feast day.

دار
Part I · Discovery — The name, word one

“Dar” — the house

دار — البيت الذي يحتضن ضيوفه

In Morocco a dar is not just a building. It is the courtyard house — the riad — where the family gathers, the table is always long, and the door is always open.

Choosing “Dar” instead of “restaurant” repositions the entire brand: guests are not customers, they are invited into a home. This single word drives the ornamental frame of the logo — an architectural doorway that shelters everything inside it.

The House · Darالدار
بهجة
Part I · Discovery — The name, word two

“Albahja” — the joy

البهجة — لقب مراكش نفسها

Albahja means joy — and for centuries it has been the beloved nickname of Marrakech itself: Madinat al-Bahja, the City of Joy.

The name is therefore a double signature: it promises an emotion and names the city of origin in the same breath. That is why “MARRAKECH” sits inside the lockup in saffron gold — not as an address, but as a birthplace. مدينة البهجة — مراكش

Heritage — التراث

Centuries of Moroccan craft — zellige tilework, carved cedar, hammered brass — translated into a logo built from the same geometric grammar. Heritage is why the mark uses real construction geometry, not decoration.

Part IV · The Assembly — Under the hood

Hand-drawn face,
engineered bones

وجه مرسوم باليد وهيكل مهندس

The ogee frame is constructed on 45° guides with mirrored quadrants; the tagine sits on the exact vertical axis; the stars are placed on radial divisions of the wreath circle. This is why the emblem stays crisp from a wax seal to a five-metre facade.

Scales from 12 mm stamp to building signage — قابل للتكبير
Symmetrical quadrants — flawless embroidery & engraving — تناظر كامل
Clear-space rule: one star-width on all sides — مساحة أمان

Part V · System — How the colors live together

The 40 · 30 · 18 · 12 rule

قاعدة توزيع الألوان في كل تطبيق
IVORY 40% BURGUNDY 30% GREEN 18% GOLD 12%
١

Ivory is the canvas العاجي هو الأساس

40% — menus, walls, packaging fields breathe in Ivory Sand; the identity never feels crowded.

٢

Burgundy is the voice العنابي هو الصوت

30% — headlines, the wordmark, seals and key surfaces speak in Marrakech Burgundy.

٣

Green is the support الأخضر هو السند

18% — wreaths, dividers and secondary text carry Medina Green quietly underneath.

٤

Gold is the treasure الذهبي هو الكنز

12% — steam, stars and MARRAKECH glint in Saffron Gold. Rare on purpose: scarcity is luxury.

Part V · System — Typography, script two

The Arabic soul

الروح العربية — الخط أصل الاسم
Arabic · Naskh Calligraphic
دار البهجة مراكش — أهلاً وسهلاً بكم
Balanced naskh with generous bowls and confident terminals. Arabic here is not a translation — it is the origin of the name, and it reads first for every Arabic-speaking guest.

In the lockup, the naskh line sits directly beneath the serif line at a matched optical weight — two scripts, one dignity, neither subordinate to the other. حرفان بكرامة واحدة
